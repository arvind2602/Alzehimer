    • What is Alzheimer's Disease?

      Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects the brain's function and causes memory loss, cognitive impairment, and behavioral changes. It is the most common cause of dementia, accounting for 60-80% of dementia cases. The disease affects nerve cells and connections in the brain, leading to the death of brain cells and the shrinkage of brain tissue. Some of the most common symptoms of Alzheimer's disease include forgetfulness, difficulty in completing daily tasks, language problems, mood swings, and disorientation.

    • How is Alzheimer's Disease Diagnosed?

      Alzheimer's disease can be diagnosed through a combination of medical history, physical examination, and various tests. One of the most common ways to diagnose Alzheimer's disease is through a cognitive test, which evaluates a person's memory, language, and other cognitive abilities. Another common diagnostic tool is a brain scan, which includes an MRI, CT scan, or PET scan, to examine the brain for signs of Alzheimer's disease. A spinal tap, which tests the fluid in the spinal cord, can also provide useful information about Alzheimer's disease. Finally, genetic testing may also be used to diagnose Alzheimer's disease, particularly in cases where the disease runs in families.

    • What is MRI?

      MRI stands for magnetic resonance imaging. It is a non-invasive diagnostic imaging technique that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the body's internal structures. MRI is a very powerful imaging tool that can provide detailed images of the brain, spinal cord, bones, and other soft tissues. MRI is used for various medical conditions such as cancer, multiple sclerosis, and neurological disorders, including Alzheimer's disease. The MRI machine is a large, tube-like structure that patients lie in while the machine takes images of their body. The MRI machine is operated by a trained technician, and the procedure is painless and usually takes between 30-60 minutes.

    • How Does MRI Help Detect Alzheimer's Disease?

      MRI is a useful tool in the detection of Alzheimer's disease because it can provide detailed images of the brain's structure and function. In Alzheimer's disease, there is a loss of brain tissue, particularly in the hippocampus, which is responsible for memory formation. MRI can detect changes in the hippocampus and other brain regions, such as the cerebral cortex, that are affected by Alzheimer's disease. MRI can also detect the presence of amyloid plaques and neurofibrillary tangles, which are the hallmark signs of Alzheimer's disease.
